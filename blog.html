How I Started My Web Dev Journey

I began exploring web development during my first year at Cavite State University. At first, HTML and CSS felt like a foreign language, but I was determined to learn. I spent hours experimenting with layouts, colors, and structure. Over time, I realized that coding is more than just syntax — it's a creative tool that lets me build ideas from scratch.

Designing My Portfolio

Creating my portfolio was both exciting and challenging. I wanted it to reflect who I am — a developer with a passion for clean design and functionality. I focused on responsive layouts, intuitive navigation, and a touch of personality. Every section, from “About Me” to “Projects,” was crafted to tell my story and showcase my growth.

Lessons from My First JavaScript Project

My first JavaScript project was a simple to-do list app. It taught me how to interact with the DOM, handle user input, and debug errors. I learned that even small projects can teach big lessons. The experience gave me confidence and sparked my interest in building more interactive features.

Why I Love Web Design

Web design lets me blend logic with creativity. I enjoy choosing color palettes, experimenting with typography, and creating layouts that feel intuitive. It’s like solving a puzzle where every piece — code, design, user experience — fits together to form something meaningful.
